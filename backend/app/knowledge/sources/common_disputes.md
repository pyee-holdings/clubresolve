# Common Disputes Between Parents and BC Sports Clubs

## Billing and Financial Disputes

### Unexpected Fees
- Clubs charge additional fees not disclosed at registration (tournament fees, equipment fees, travel surcharges).
- Resolution: Request itemized invoice. Compare to registration materials and website. Check club bylaws for fee-setting authority.

### Refund Disputes
- Parent requests refund after athlete withdraws (injury, relocation, dissatisfaction).
- Many clubs have no-refund or partial-refund policies. Check: Was the policy disclosed at registration? Is it in the membership agreement? Is it reasonable?
- Resolution: Review membership agreement for refund terms. If no policy exists or it wasn't disclosed, the parent has a stronger case.

### Financial Transparency
- Parents question how fees are spent, especially for large fundraising or special assessments.
- Under the BC Societies Act, members can request financial statements and inspect records.
- Resolution: Submit written request for financial statements citing Section 20-23 of the BC Societies Act.

### Unauthorized Charges
- Club charges parent's credit card without clear authorization for specific amounts.
- Resolution: Dispute with payment provider if charge was unauthorized. Document the authorization (or lack thereof).

## Safety Concerns

### Coaching Behavior
- Verbal abuse, excessive physical demands, inappropriate conduct.
- This is a SafeSport issue. Follow SafeSport escalation path.
- Resolution: Document incidents with dates, witnesses. Report to club SafeSport officer. If unresolved, escalate to PSO.

### Training Conditions
- Unsafe facilities, inadequate supervision, training in extreme weather.
- Resolution: Document conditions (photos, dates). Report to club board in writing. If risk is immediate, remove athlete and report to facility owner or municipal authority.

### Injury Response
- Club fails to properly respond to injury, lacks first aid, pressures injured athletes to continue.
- Resolution: Document the incident. Report to club and governing body. If negligent, consult a personal injury lawyer.

### Rule of Two Violations
- Coach alone with minor athlete without proper safeguards.
- Resolution: Report immediately to club SafeSport officer. Document date, time, circumstances. Escalate to PSO if club does not act.

## Governance Disputes

### Board Decision-Making
- Board makes decisions without proper process, conflicts of interest, or member consultation.
- Under the BC Societies Act, directors must act in the best interests of the society and disclose conflicts.
- Resolution: Attend AGM. Request meeting minutes. If improper conduct, file complaint with BC Registrar.

### Bylaw Violations
- Club does not follow its own bylaws (e.g., discipline without proper process, elections not held properly).
- Resolution: Obtain copy of bylaws. Document the violation. Raise at general meeting or file complaint with Registrar.

### Lack of Transparency
- Board refuses to share information, doesn't hold AGMs, doesn't provide financial statements.
- Resolution: Exercise member rights under BC Societies Act. Requisition a general meeting if needed (10% of members).

## Team Selection and Playing Time

### Tryout Fairness
- Parent believes tryout evaluation was biased or not conducted according to stated criteria.
- Resolution: Request the club's published tryout criteria. Ask for evaluation results. Document concerns in writing.

### Playing Time
- Athlete not receiving playing time or opportunities promised.
- Note: Playing time decisions are generally at coach's discretion. Stronger case if specific promises were made in writing.
- Resolution: Document any written promises. Discuss with coach first. Escalate to club director of coaching.

### Eligibility
- Athlete prevented from playing due to administrative issues (registration, transfer, fees).
- Resolution: Verify registration status with club and governing body. Address any outstanding administrative issues. Request timeline for resolution.

## Communication Issues

### Unresponsive Club
- Club fails to respond to emails, calls, or requests.
- Resolution: Send communications in writing (email). Keep records. Escalate to board if staff unresponsive. Set clear timelines.

### Bullying and Social Dynamics
- Athlete experiences bullying from teammates, with inadequate club response.
- Resolution: Report in writing to coach and club. If SafeSport issue, follow SafeSport process. Document incidents.

## Escalation Paths by Issue Type

### Financial Issues
1. Direct discussion with treasurer/registrar
2. Written complaint to club board
3. Request financial records under BC Societies Act
4. Small claims court (for amounts under $5,000 in BC)

### Safety Issues
1. Report to club SafeSport officer
2. Escalate to provincial sport organization
3. Report to viaSport BC
4. Police/MCFD for criminal/child welfare matters

### Governance Issues
1. Raise at general meeting
2. Written complaint to board
3. Requisition general meeting (10% of members)
4. Complain to BC Registrar of Societies
5. BC Supreme Court application (serious cases)

### Coaching/Team Selection
1. Discussion with coach
2. Escalate to club technical director
3. Written complaint to board
4. Provincial sport organization appeal process
