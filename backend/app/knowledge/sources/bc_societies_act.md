# BC Societies Act — Key Provisions for Sports Clubs

## Overview
The BC Societies Act (SBC 2015, c. 18) governs non-profit societies in British Columbia, including most amateur sports clubs. It sets out the rights of members, obligations of directors, and rules for governance.

## Member Rights

### Right to Information (Section 20-23)
- Members have the right to inspect the society's register of members, register of directors, financial statements, and minutes of general meetings.
- A member may request copies of these records, and the society must provide them within 14 days.
- The society may charge a reasonable fee for copies.
- Members do NOT have an automatic right to inspect minutes of directors' meetings (board meetings).
- The society must keep records at its registered office or another location in BC approved by the directors.
- If a society refuses access to records, the member can apply to court for an order compelling access.

### Right to Receive Notice (Section 76-78)
- Members must receive at least 14 days' notice of a general meeting (or as specified in bylaws).
- Notice must state the date, time, and location of the meeting.
- If a special resolution will be proposed, the full text of the resolution must be included in the notice.
- Notice can be given by mail, email, or other method specified in the bylaws.
- Failure to give proper notice can invalidate any decisions made at the meeting.

### Right to Requisition a Meeting (Section 79)
- If 10% or more of voting members sign a requisition, the directors must call a general meeting within 21 days.
- The requisition must state the business to be conducted.
- If directors fail to call the meeting, the requisitioners can call it themselves.
- The society must reimburse reasonable costs of a requisitioned meeting.

### Right to Attend and Vote at General Meetings (Section 75-82)
- Every member in good standing has the right to attend general meetings.
- Members have the right to vote on ordinary and special resolutions.
- A special resolution requires 2/3 of votes cast.
- Members can requisition a general meeting if at least 10% of voting members sign the requisition.

### Right to Receive Financial Statements (Section 35-37)
- The society must present financial statements at every annual general meeting (AGM).
- For societies with annual revenue over $500,000, financial statements must be reviewed or audited by a qualified accountant.
- Members can request copies of the most recent financial statements.

## Director Obligations

### Duty of Care (Section 53)
- Directors must act honestly and in good faith with a view to the best interests of the society.
- Directors must exercise the care, diligence, and skill of a reasonably prudent person.
- Directors can rely in good faith on information provided by officers, employees, or professionals.
- This duty is owed to the society as a whole, not to individual members.

### Conflict of Interest (Section 56-58)
- Directors must disclose any material interest in a contract or transaction.
- A director with a conflict must not vote on the matter.
- Failure to disclose conflicts can result in personal liability.
- Disclosure must be made at the first board meeting after the director becomes aware of the conflict.
- The disclosure must be recorded in the minutes of the meeting.

### Financial Accountability
- Directors are responsible for ensuring proper accounting records are maintained.
- Directors must approve financial statements before they are presented to members.
- Financial statements must be prepared in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) or disclosed standards.

### Removal of Directors (Section 52)
- Members can remove a director before their term expires by ordinary resolution (majority vote) at a general meeting.
- The director must be given notice and an opportunity to address the members before the vote.
- A director removed by members cannot be re-appointed by the remaining directors until the next AGM.

### Director Liability (Section 54-55)
- Directors who authorize improper payments, distributions, or loans may be jointly and severally liable.
- Directors can be personally liable for unpaid employee wages (up to 2 months).
- Directors are not liable if they relied in good faith on financial statements or professional advice.

## Bylaws

### Bylaw Requirements (Section 11-12)
- Every society must have bylaws.
- Bylaws must not conflict with the Societies Act or the society's constitution.
- Default bylaws (Schedule 1) apply if a society has not adopted its own.

### Amending Bylaws (Section 16)
- Bylaws can only be amended by special resolution (2/3 vote at a general meeting).
- Members must receive adequate notice of proposed bylaw changes.
- Amended bylaws must be filed with the BC Registrar.

## Dispute Resolution

### Internal Dispute Resolution
- Many societies include dispute resolution procedures in their bylaws.
- If bylaws are silent, the society should follow principles of procedural fairness.
- Members who are disciplined or expelled must be given:
  - Written notice of the allegations
  - An opportunity to be heard
  - A decision that is not made in bad faith

### Complaints to the Registrar (Section 99)
- The Registrar of Societies can investigate complaints about a society's compliance with the Act.
- Complaints can be filed if the society is not following its own bylaws or the Act.

### Court Applications (Section 100-101)
- A member can apply to the BC Supreme Court if the society's affairs are being conducted in a manner that is oppressive or unfairly prejudicial.
- The court has broad powers to remedy oppressive conduct.

## Membership

### Membership Rights (Section 40-42)
- A society must maintain a register of members.
- Members can only be expelled or disciplined in accordance with the bylaws.
- If the bylaws do not address discipline, the common law principles of procedural fairness apply.

### Fees and Dues
- Membership fees must be set in accordance with the bylaws.
- Members should receive clear notice of fee changes.
- A society cannot retroactively change fees without member approval.

## Annual General Meetings (AGMs)

### Requirements (Section 75-77)
- Every society must hold an AGM within 15 months of its last AGM.
- At least 10% of members must receive notice of the AGM, though best practice is to notify all members.
- The AGM agenda must include: financial statements, election of directors, appointment of auditor (if required).

### Member Participation
- Members have the right to ask questions at AGMs.
- Members can propose resolutions if proper notice requirements are met.
- Proxy voting is allowed if the bylaws permit it.

## Procedural Fairness in Discipline

### Principles
- Any member facing discipline or expulsion must be afforded procedural fairness (natural justice).
- This applies even if the bylaws do not explicitly require it.
- The key elements are:
  1. **Notice**: The member must receive written notice of the specific allegations against them.
  2. **Hearing**: The member must have a reasonable opportunity to respond to the allegations (in person or in writing).
  3. **Impartial decision-maker**: The person(s) deciding the matter must not have a personal interest or bias.
  4. **Reasons**: The member should receive written reasons for any adverse decision.
  5. **Appeal**: Best practice is to provide an appeal mechanism.

### Common Procedural Failures in Sports Clubs
- Board member who made the complaint also votes on the discipline decision.
- Member not told what specific rule or policy they allegedly violated.
- Decision made before the member has a chance to respond.
- No written record of the decision or reasons.
- Discipline imposed without a meeting or hearing.
- Excessive punishment disproportionate to the alleged misconduct.

## Sample Club Bylaw Provisions

### Typical Discipline Clause
"The Board of Directors may, by resolution, suspend or expel a member for conduct detrimental to the purposes of the Society, provided that:
(a) the member is given 14 days' written notice of the proposed discipline, including the specific grounds;
(b) the member is given an opportunity to be heard by the Board;
(c) the Board's decision is communicated in writing within 7 days;
(d) the member may appeal to the membership at the next general meeting."

### Typical Complaint Procedure
"Any member may file a written complaint with the Board of Directors. The Board shall acknowledge receipt within 7 days and provide a written response within 30 days. If the complainant is not satisfied, they may request the matter be placed on the agenda of the next general meeting."

### Typical Fee/Refund Clause
"Membership fees are set annually by the Board or by resolution at the AGM. Fees paid are non-refundable except: (a) if the program is cancelled by the Society; (b) for medical reasons with documentation; (c) at the discretion of the Board."

## viaSport BC Framework

### Role
- viaSport is BC's lead sport organization, responsible for developing and delivering sport programs.
- viaSport provides governance support, policy templates, and dispute resolution guidance to clubs.
- viaSport administers the BC Sport Participation Program and other funding.

### Club Accountability
- Clubs receiving viaSport funding must comply with viaSport policies.
- viaSport can investigate complaints about club governance.
- Non-compliance may result in loss of funding or recognition.

### Safe Sport Requirements
- All clubs must have a written Code of Conduct.
- All clubs must have a complaint handling process.
- Coaches and volunteers in positions of trust must complete Respect in Sport training or equivalent.
- viaSport endorses the Universal Code of Conduct to Prevent and Address Maltreatment in Sport (UCCMS).
