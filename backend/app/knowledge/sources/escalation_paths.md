# Escalation Paths for BC Sports Club Disputes

## Overview
Escalation should be progressive — start with the lowest-conflict approach and escalate only when lower levels fail. Acting too aggressively too early can damage relationships and reduce the chance of a good outcome.

## Level 0: Direct Communication

### Approach
- Speak or write directly to the person involved (coach, administrator, treasurer).
- Keep communication professional and specific.
- State the issue clearly, what outcome you want, and a reasonable timeline.

### When to Use
- First attempt at resolving any issue.
- When the issue may be a misunderstanding or administrative error.

### Tips
- Put it in writing (email) so there's a record.
- Be specific about dates, amounts, and incidents.
- Ask for a response within a specific timeframe (e.g., 10 business days).

## Level 1: Formal Written Complaint to Club Board

### Approach
- Write a formal complaint letter to the club's board of directors.
- Reference specific bylaws, policies, or agreements that are relevant.
- Attach supporting evidence.
- Request a written response within a specified timeframe.

### When to Use
- Direct communication was unsuccessful or ignored.
- The issue is with a staff member and needs board attention.
- The issue involves a policy violation.

### Tips
- Address the letter to the board president or secretary.
- Send by email with a read receipt, or by registered mail.
- Keep a copy of everything sent and received.

## Level 2: Provincial Sport Organization (PSO)

### Approach
- File a complaint with the sport's provincial governing body.
- Most PSOs have formal complaint or appeal procedures.
- The PSO may investigate, mediate, or impose sanctions on the club.

### When to Use
- Club has not resolved the issue after a formal complaint.
- The issue involves safety or policy violations that the PSO governs.
- The club is refusing to follow its own policies or the PSO's policies.

### Key PSOs in BC
- BC Soccer Association
- BC Hockey
- Gymnastics BC
- BC Athletics
- Swim BC
- Field Hockey BC
- BC Lacrosse Association
- BC Rugby
- viaSport BC (umbrella organization)

### Tips
- Review the PSO's specific complaint procedure before filing.
- Include all documentation from previous resolution attempts.
- PSO processes can take weeks to months — plan accordingly.

## Level 3: External Bodies

### Sport Dispute Resolution Centre of Canada (SDRCC)
- National body for sport disputes.
- Provides mediation and arbitration services.
- Handles appeals from PSO decisions.
- Website: www.crdsc-sdrcc.ca

### BC Registrar of Societies
- Investigates complaints about societies not complying with the BC Societies Act.
- Can order a society to comply with its bylaws or the Act.
- File a complaint at: www.gov.bc.ca/societies

### BC Human Rights Tribunal
- If the dispute involves discrimination based on a protected ground (race, disability, gender, etc.).
- Must file within one year of the alleged discrimination.
- Website: www.bchrt.bc.ca

### Small Claims Court
- For financial disputes under $5,000 (BC).
- Relatively quick and inexpensive process.
- Can be used for refund disputes, breach of contract (membership agreement).
- Website: www.smallclaimsbc.ca

### BC Supreme Court
- For serious governance issues (oppression remedy under BC Societies Act).
- Expensive and time-consuming — last resort.
- Consider consulting a lawyer before this step.

### Police / MCFD
- For criminal matters (assault, theft, fraud).
- For child welfare concerns (abuse, neglect).
- MCFD after-hours line: 1-800-663-9122
- Do NOT wait for internal sport processes when safety is at risk.

## Choosing the Right Level

### Factors to Consider
1. **Severity**: Safety issues may skip directly to police/MCFD.
2. **Urgency**: Time-sensitive issues (e.g., upcoming competition) may need faster escalation.
3. **Evidence strength**: Escalate only when you have sufficient documentation.
4. **Relationship preservation**: Lower levels preserve relationships better.
5. **Cost**: Higher levels (lawyer, court) are more expensive.
6. **Precedent**: Has the club resolved similar issues before?

### Red Flags for Immediate Escalation
- Physical abuse or sexual misconduct → Police immediately
- Child welfare concerns → MCFD immediately
- Imminent safety risk → Remove athlete, then report
- Retaliation for reporting → Escalate to PSO and document everything

## Documentation Best Practices

### What to Keep
- All emails and written communications
- Screenshots of text messages or social media posts
- Photos of conditions or injuries
- Financial records (invoices, receipts, payment records)
- Bylaws, policies, and membership agreements
- Meeting minutes and agendas
- Witness statements (dated and signed)
- Timeline of events

### How to Organize
- Chronological order
- Label each document with date, type, and relevance
- Create a summary document that indexes all evidence
- Keep originals safe; use copies for submissions
