# SafeSport Policies — Athlete Safety in BC Sports

## Overview
SafeSport policies protect athletes from abuse, harassment, and discrimination in sport. In BC, these policies are driven by viaSport BC, the provincial sport organization, and implemented by individual sport governing bodies and clubs.

## viaSport BC SafeSport Framework

### Universal Code of Conduct to Prevent and Address Maltreatment in Sport (UCCMS)
- Adopted by Sport Canada and cascaded through provincial sport organizations.
- Defines maltreatment categories: psychological, physical, sexual, neglect, grooming.
- Applies to all individuals in sport: coaches, officials, administrators, volunteers, athletes, parents.

### Reporting Obligations
- Anyone who witnesses or suspects maltreatment has a duty to report.
- Reports of child abuse must also be made to the Ministry of Children and Family Development (MCFD) or police.
- Sport organizations must have independent complaint mechanisms — they cannot investigate themselves for serious allegations.

### Independent Investigation
- Serious complaints (abuse, harassment, discrimination) should be referred to an independent third party.
- The Sport Dispute Resolution Centre of Canada (SDRCC) provides investigation services.
- Clubs should not conduct internal investigations of serious complaints.

## Athlete Rights

### Right to a Safe Environment
- Athletes have the right to participate in sport free from abuse, harassment, and discrimination.
- Clubs must maintain policies that create and sustain safe sport environments.

### Right to Report Without Retaliation
- Athletes and parents who file complaints must not face retaliation.
- Retaliation includes: removal from team, reduced playing time, social exclusion, or any adverse action related to the complaint.

### Right to Due Process
- Accused individuals are presumed innocent until a fair process determines otherwise.
- Both complainants and respondents have the right to be heard.

## Parent Rights and Responsibilities

### Right to Information
- Parents have the right to know what SafeSport policies are in place at their child's club.
- Clubs should make SafeSport policies publicly available.

### Right to Complain
- Parents can file complaints on behalf of their children.
- Complaints should be directed to the club's designated SafeSport officer first, then escalated to the governing body if unresolved.

### Escalation Path for SafeSport Concerns
1. Report to club SafeSport officer or designate
2. If unresolved or if the club is the subject of the complaint: report to the provincial sport organization (PSO)
3. If unresolved at PSO level: report to viaSport BC
4. For criminal matters: report to police immediately (do not wait for internal processes)
5. For child welfare matters: report to MCFD at 1-800-663-9122

## Coach Screening and Certification

### Criminal Record Checks
- All coaches working with minors should have a current criminal record check.
- BC requires a Criminal Record Check through the Criminal Records Review Program (CRRP) for individuals working with children and vulnerable adults.

### Coaching Certification
- Coaches should have appropriate NCCP (National Coaching Certification Program) certification.
- Sport governing bodies set minimum certification requirements.

### Rule of Two
- The Rule of Two requires that there always be two screened adults present during interactions with minor athletes.
- This applies to training, travel, and any one-on-one situations.
- Exceptions must be documented and approved.

## Club Obligations

### Policy Requirements
- Clubs must have written SafeSport policies.
- Policies must include: code of conduct, complaint procedures, investigation procedures, disciplinary sanctions.
- Policies must be communicated to all members (athletes, parents, coaches, volunteers).

### Training
- Clubs should provide SafeSport training for all coaches and volunteers.
- The Coaching Association of Canada offers SafeSport training modules.

### Record Keeping
- Clubs must maintain records of complaints, investigations, and outcomes.
- Records must be kept confidential and stored securely.
